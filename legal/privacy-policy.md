## 2. Information we collect

**Information you provide to us:**

- **Account information** — your name, email address, company name, role, and password (stored only as a securely hashed value).
- **Profile and team information** — details you add about your organisation and the people you invite.
- **Project and operational data** — projects, tasks, checklists, budgets, cost codes, commitments, invoices, bids and tenders, inventory and equipment records, approvals, comments, and any files, photos, or documents you upload.
- **Billing information** — your subscription plan and payment records. **Card and mobile‑money details are collected and processed directly by our payment provider (Paystack); we do not store your full card number or M‑Pesa PIN.**
- **Communications** — messages you send to our support channels, including the Shemmy support chat, WhatsApp, and email.

**Information we collect automatically:**

- **Usage data** — how you interact with the Service, such as features used, pages viewed, and actions taken.
- **Device and log data** — IP address, browser type, device information, and timestamps.
- **Cookies and similar technologies** — used to keep you signed in and to operate the Service. See Section 8.

**Information from third parties:**

- **Google Sign‑In** — if you choose "Continue with Google", we receive your name, email address, and profile picture from Google to create or access your account. We do not access your Gmail, Google Drive, or contacts.

## 3. How we use your information

We use your information to:

- provide, operate, and maintain the Service;
- create and manage your account and workspace;
- process subscriptions and payments through our payment provider;
- send transactional emails such as verification links, password resets, invoices, and notifications;
- provide customer support and respond to your requests;
- monitor, secure, and improve the Service, and develop new features;
- detect, prevent, and address fraud, abuse, or technical issues; and
- comply with legal obligations.

We rely on the following legal bases under the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019: performance of our contract with you, your consent (which you may withdraw), our legitimate business interests, and compliance with legal obligations.

**We do not sell your personal data, and we do not use your project data to train third‑party AI models.**

## 4. How we share information

We share information only as needed to run the Service:

- **Within your workspace** — data you enter is visible to other authorised members of your company workspace according to their roles and permissions.
- **Service providers (sub‑processors)** who act on our behalf, including:
  - **Railway** — application hosting and database infrastructure.
  - **Paystack** — payment processing (cards and M‑Pesa).
  - **Resend** — sending transactional and notification emails.
  - **Google** — authentication when you use Google Sign‑In.
  - AI and analytics providers used to power in‑product assistance and support features.
- **Legal and safety** — where required by law, court order, or regulator, or to protect the rights, safety, and property of Buildsasa, our users, or the public.
- **Business transfers** — if Buildsasa is involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to this Policy.

We require our service providers to protect your information and to use it only for the purposes we specify.

## 7. Your rights

Subject to the Kenya Data Protection Act, 2019, you have the right to:

- **access** the personal data we hold about you;
- **correct** inaccurate or incomplete data;
- **request deletion** of your data ("right to be forgotten");
- **object to or restrict** certain processing;
- **withdraw consent** where processing is based on consent; and
- **data portability** — receive a copy of your data in a commonly used format.

To exercise any of these rights, email **hello@buildsasa.com**. We will respond within the timeframes required by law. If you believe we have not handled your data properly, you may lodge a complaint with the **Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC)** in Kenya.
